How to Make Rose Cherise Eclairs

ingredients

  • 75ml (about 5 tablespoons) of milk
  • 1 teaspoon caster sugar
  • 60g (about 1/4 of a cup) of unsalted butter chopped
  • 100g (2/3 cup) plain flour, sifted
  • 3 eggs at room temperature
  • 200g (4/6 of a cup) mascarpone
  • 200ml (13.5 tablespoons) pure (thin) cream

      • Rose cherise glaze

    1. 2tbs glucose syrup(from supermarket)
    2. 1 tsp rosewater
    3. 50g (about 1/4 a cup) frozen cherries
    4. 350g (about 2 3/4 cups) pure icing sugar
    5. 2-3 drops red food dye
    6. dried rose petals

Directions

  1. preheat oven to 200 degrees celsius(about 390 degrees fahrenheit). Grease 3 large baking trays and line with baking paper.

  2. To make the coux pastrey, place milk, sugar, butter 75ml(about 5 tablespoons) of water and a pinch of salt flakes in a saucepan over medium heat. Cook, stirring, for 2-3 minuets or until butter is melted and foaming.
    Add flour, stirring to bring together into a dough. Using a wooden spoon, beat douh consistanly for 5 minuets or until catching on base of saucepan and coming away from the sides.

  3. Transfer to a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment and beat for 2 minuets or until loosened slightly. Increase the speed to medium-high and add the eggs, 1 at a time and making sure each egg is fully encorperated before adding the next, until smooth.

  4. Transfer the choux pastry to a piping bag fitted with a 1.2cm round nozzle and pipe 12cm lengths across prepared baking trays, leaving 3cm between each. Bake for 10 minuets, then reduce oven to 180 degrees celsius
    (about 360 degrees fahrenheit) and bake, swapping the top and bottom trays halfway, for a further 15 minuets or until puffed and golden. Remove from the oven and cool on trays for 10 minuets.

  5. Using a serrated knife, cut the cooled pasteris in half horizontally. Return tops and bases, cut-side up, to trays and bake for a further 10 minuets or until centers are dry to the touch. Cool.

  6. For the rose cherise glaze, place glucose, rosewater and cherries in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to the boil, breaking up with a wooden spoon. Strain, discarding solids, then set aside.

  7. Sift icing sugar into a bowl, making a well in the center. Add strained cherry liquid, stir to combine then add water, 1 teaspoon at a time and stirring constantly, until a smooth, loose consistency.
    Add food dye, 1 drop at a time, to achieve a soft pink color. Dip tops of pasteries into the glaze, then set aside on a wire rack over baking-paper-lined baking tray. Scatter the rose petals and allow to stand for 10 minuets or until set.

  8. When ready to serve, place mascarpone and cream in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the whisk attatchment. Whisk on high speed to stiff peaks. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a 1.2cm round nozzle.
    Pipe rounds of marscarpone cream along the center of each base, then top with the glazed tops to serve.
